Breaking Down Barriers

Education for Encouragment Into a Stem Field

Introduction

This webpage is to educate men and women on the lack of representation women have in STEM fields. We also want to encourage younger women to pursue careers in STEM. Below you will find:

  1. Facts on biases with women in STEM
  2. Current steps taken to improve women representation is STEM
  3. Statisics on the differences in gender
  4. Activities to prove that anyone can learn to be in the STEM field
  5. And a call to action

Biases

Though their numbers are growing, only 27% of all students taking the AP Computer Science exam in the United States are female.

This is in the Unites States, where many college men proudly describe themselves as "male feminists" and girls are taught they can be anything they want to be.

Jordan, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates were the only three countries in which boys are significantly less likely to feel comfortable working on math problems than girls are. In all of the other nations surveyed, girls were more likely to say they feel helpless while performing a math problem.

In the United States, women in computing, engineering, and science occupations were paid an estimated 79.2% of men's annual median earnings in 2016.

The percentages of people with top secret security clearances are approximately 90% male and 10% female.

Work experiences impact women's decisions to leave. Isolation, hostile male-dominated work enviornment, ineffective executive feedback, and a lack of effective sponsors are factors pushing women to leave Science, Engineering, and Technology (SET) jobs.

Percent of women who intend to leave their SET jobs within a year:

United States 32%
China 30%
Brazil 22%
India 20%

Women are significantly under-represented in high-tech occupations. In 2016, women accounted for one-fifth or less of those employed in some of these jobs.

PERCENT OF WOMEN IN EACH FIELD:

Computer network architects 10%
Aerospace Engineers 8%

Combating these Biases

Even through we as a society still have a long way to go, some organizations have taken steps to address women's under-representation.

Some of the best ways to encourage young women are to:

  1. Expose girls to STEM early
  2. Provide learning opportunities outside of the classroom
  3. Special Programs
  4. Provide female mentors in varied STEM fields

Here are some examples of ways to encourage young girls. There are many more but these are good examples.

Grace Hopper

more_vert

Grace Hopper Conference

Grace Hopper Conferenceclose Grace Hopper Conference

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was an American computer scientist and United States Navy rear admiral. One of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer, she was a pioneer of computer programming who invented one of the first compiler related tools. The Grace Hopper Celebration is an annual conference that is the world's largest gathering of women technologists.

Call to Action

Hopefully with these resources, young girls will be encouraged to:

  1. Become interested in STEM early
  2. Join outside STEM learning opportunities
  3. Join STEM Special Programs provided in their communities or schools
  4. Or ask for a STEM mentor to help guide them in their educational adventure

We hope that one day these statistics will be drastically different and that the biases towards women in STEM will no longer exist.

We also encourage society as a whole to accept women in STEM and for current employees to make hiring decisions based on candidate's capabilities instead of their gender.
